2026-07-08 (WEDNESDAY): MACOS CLICKFIX CAMPAIGN ESTABLISHING A PERSISTENT BACKDOOR	

AUTHORS:

- Nabeel Mohamed, Billy Melicher, Alex Starov

NOTES:

- We are tracking an active MacOS ClickFix campaign that deceptively installs a persistent backdoor using macOS LaunchAgent.
- First observed on the 3rd of July 2026
- The campaign has been spinning up one or two lure hostnames abusing a free hosting service.
- All clipboard payloads have the same bash piped base64 encoded payload. 
  -- Decoded payloads contain the second stage payload with the format:
     <random_subdomain>.<low_reputed_doman>//update.sh 
  -- This suggests that the campaign is being generated using a kit.
- We observed two distinct lure templates used across the campaign: 
  1. A page mimicking a CAPTCHA-style challenge with "Safe Access Confirmation"
  2. A page mimicking another challenge with "VaultPass/Secure Identity Gateway"
- The second stage payload has three-layer nesting with base64 encoding:
  -- bash
  -- osascript 
  -- AppleScript shell command
- Three of the payload URL domains are hosting similar but low-reputed betting sites.
  -- We found fake reviews and affiliate marketing redirection links in them. 

ATTACK CHAIN DETAILS:

- The victim is attracted to lure pages via paid ad redirect through grabora[.]org or search (Example campaign ID 11209365). 
- MacOS is the tracked platform in the redirector URL.
- Victim lands on a page (<subdomain>.pages[.]dev) presenting one of the two CAPTCHA templates.
- Victim clicks the CAPTCHA checkbox, which writes the bash payload to the clipboard. 
  -- Then transitions to "Verifying…" and then displays the macOS terminal instructions.
- Victim follows the on-screen instruction, which decodes and executes the remote bash script named update.sh.
- Update.sh installs a persistent backdoor on the victim's machine using the macOS osascript command.
  -- This wraps a base64-encoded AppleScript that writes a per-victim LaunchAgent plist.
- Stage 3 payload gets executed on each user login. 
  -- The exact capabilities of stage 3 remain unconfirmed without sandbox execution of the payload.
