2026-07-02 (THURSDAY): CLICKFIX CAMPAIGN UTILIZING A MAAS KIT WITH BLOCKCHAIN C2	

AUTHORS:

- Nabeel Mohamed  Billy Melicher Oleksii Alex Starov

NOTES:

- We are tracking an active Malware-as-a-Service (MaaS) operation abusing Polygon (MATIC) blockchain as a resilient C2 configuration with s ClickFix lure.
- The campaign has been active for the last 28 days and we have detected 130+ compromised lure websites.
- Compromised websites are injected with a script named tracker.js appearing as "JokerStat Analytics Tracker". 
- In addition to the clipboard injection, this script performs screenshot and victim-session telemetry exfiltration every 2 minutes.
- All 15 C2 domains run a full operator-facing web application with the same "JokerStat Analytics" identity.
- WHOIS registration records suggest that all the C2 domains were registered very recently in two distinct batches in May and June 2026. 
  -- Further, C2 domains are registered under low reputed TLDs such as .sbs, .click and .lat.
- Passive DNS records show that these C2 collectively received over 10K queries during this short time period, indicating the widespread nature of the attack.

DETAILS:

- Attack Chain:
  -- The threat actor compromises WordPress sites to inject tracker.js delivery chain.
  -- A victim visits a compromised site and tracker.js performs a blockchain lookup to fetch the C2 server URL which is base36-encoded.
  -- After C2 resolution, tracker.js starts collecting victim telemetry including pageview events, heartbeat and screenshots.
  -- The same tracker.js then injects the clipboard content personalized per victim. 
     --- The victim sees a fake CAPTCHA overlay and follows the instruction leading to a ClickFix attack.
  -- The clipboard content contains the first-stage dropper
     --- The first-stage dropper downloads and executes a second-stage powershell script.
     --- Finally, stage 3 drops an infostealer in the form of a password-protected .7z archive, written in Ruby.
- Google search on "JokerStat" returned a top hit pointing to rhumbshop[.]shop.
  -- The results suggest that this MaaS kit is deployed more widely than the 15 C2 domains identified in this campaign.
- There are actually four independent C2 resolution layers: 
  -- 1. Polygon blockchain inline script
  -- 2. tracker.js data-api-host attribute
  -- 3. DNS TXT at crf[.]masterklass[.]net
  -- 4. GitHub hosted file
