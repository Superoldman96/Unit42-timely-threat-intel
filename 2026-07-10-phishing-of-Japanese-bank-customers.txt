2026-07-10 (FRIDAY): FAKE "POINT EXPIRATION REMINDER" PHISHING IMPERSONATING JAPANESE FINANCIAL INSTITUTIONS.

AUTHOR:

- Amanda Tanner

NOTES:

- Unit 42 has discovered an active credential-harvesting phishing campaign.
- This campaign is impersonating Japanese financial institutions.
- Attackers deployed single-page phishing sites to dynamically serve fake "point expiration" alerts.
- These pages urge users to click a "Next" (次へ) button.
- The goal is to steal the login credentials of customers. 
- The infrastructure relies on an automated Domain Generation Algorithm (DGA) network.
- The DGA network generates typo-squatted subdomains.
  -- These subdomains are attached to randomized or spoofed Japanese root domains to evade standard blocklists.
