2026-07-06 (MONDAY): PHISHING CAMPAIGN IMPERSONATING US STATE GOVERNMENT MOTOR VEHICLE AGENCIES

AUTHOR:

- Amanda Tanner

NOTES:

- An active phishing campaign is impersonating US state government and motor vehicle agencies and generic state-government portals.
  -- Across at least 23 US jurisdictions.
- The operator uses a consistent <state/agency>.<gov|org>-<random>.<tld> domain-naming scheme across .one/.shop/.cc/.help/.click, bulk-registered in waves from 2026-03-31 onward. 
- The .one core is fronted a hosting service to mask the origin, while a parallel .click/.help set renders live from 47.236.11[.]46.
- Same-operator attribution is anchored by shared WHOIS registrant tokens, a shared turnstile sitekey (confirmed on a subset), and a dyna-ns[.]net nameserver bridging to AS135392.
- Dozens of hostnames are individually verified within a larger cluster of several hundred similarly-registered domains.
