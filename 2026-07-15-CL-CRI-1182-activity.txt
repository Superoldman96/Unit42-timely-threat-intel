2026-07-15 (WEDNESDAY): CL-CRI-1182 ACTIVITY (ASSOCIATED WITH "FALCON" GROUP)

AUTHORS:

Richard Emerson, Cuong Dinh

NOTES:

- Unit 42 recently investigated cases where attackers identified themselves in their extortion email as FALCON.
- Unit 42 tracks this as activity cluster CL-CRI-1182.
- Several indicators from the CL-CRI-1182 cluster overlap with CL-CRI-1116.
- Therefore, we assess with moderate confidence that CL-CRI-1182 is related to CL-CRI-1116.
- The CL-CRI-1116 overlaps with public reporting on a group that calls itself BlackFile.
  -- More info on CL-CRI-1116 at: https://unit42.paloaltonetworks.com/cyber-extortion-economy/
  -- More info on BlackFile at: https://cloud.google.com/blog/topics/threat-intelligence/blackfile-vishing-extortion-operation

FACTORS COMMON TO BOTH CL-CRI-1182 AND CL-CRI-1116:

- CL-CRI-1182 features a vishing playbook similar to that from CL-CRI-1116.
  -- These attacks utilize passkey-themed domains.
  -- Attackers exfiltrate SaaS data (SharePoint, OneDrive, Box, etc.).
  -- Attackers use a compromised email account to send their extortion notes.
- CL-CRI-1182 features identical domain registration and hosting patterns with CL-CRI-1116.
  -- These patterns align across vishing domains for both activity clusters.
  -- This includes the use of the Nicenic registrar.
  -- The associated domains also use CloudFlare nameservers.
- Attackers associated with CL-CRI-1182 have used Mullvad VPN.
- An IP address we observed in a recent CL-CRI-1182 intrusion was also seen in a previously attributed CL-CRI-1116 case, further linking these two clusters.
