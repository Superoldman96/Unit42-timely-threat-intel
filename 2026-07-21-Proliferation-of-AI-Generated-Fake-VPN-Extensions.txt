2026-07-21 (TUESDAY): Proliferation of AI-Generated Fake VPN Extensions

Authors:

Ravindu De Silva and Nabeel Mohamed 

NOTES:

- On May 22nd 2026, we first publicly reported a threat actor operating a factory of dozens of fake VPN Chrome extensions, all routing all user browsing traffic through the same set of 15 hardcoded SOCKS5 proxy servers. (https://x.com/Unit42_Intel/status/2057860340931551588)
- Nearly two months later, the campaign is not only stil active but also creating new cloned extensions at an increasing pace. 
     - We detected over 350 new extensions so far.
- The threat actor has created at least 32 new Google Chrome Store accounts each hosting 5 - 10 extensions.
- In addition to the 15 we identified in May, some new example email accounts include noriuman26@gmail[.]com with 14 extensions and vale99505@gmail[.]com with 12 extensions.
- The number of brand impersonation targets has increased from 4 to 15.
- Only 14 extensions from this campaign were removed from the Chrome Web Store so far. All other extensions remain active and more than 3K installations are observed.
- These extensions are not providing typical encrypted protection like reputed VPN extensions and all communications occur via SOCKS5 proxy and the threat actor has access to all communications.

DETAILS:

- Proxy domains are bulk registered on a few distinct days. With notably, 6 domains on 2026-04-05 and 19 domains on 2026-05-20. 
- Most of these domains are co-hosted on two distinct hosting servers: 95[.]163.244[.]138 and 212[.]192.14[.]75. 
     - This indicates some level of automation in infrastructure provisioning. 
- Between versions 1.0.0 and 1.0.1 of extension pddhejgmgakilndagfaffgochjojogfp, the threat actor quietly added a new host permission. 
     - This change altered the core logic to dynamically fetch the QR code from a remote server, allowing them to update the QR link without needing to push a new extension release each time.
- 15 hardcoded static IPs cover US/EU nodes while operator-controlled hostnames handle JP/SG/CA/AU/TR which allow backend rotation without an extension update.
